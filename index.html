Introductory Classes for High School Girls

Open to Vancouver Secondary School Girls

Bluesoxx is offering introductory computer science classes for high school girls this May and June, in preparation for a longer course of study developed for Summer 2016 and beyond.

Girls who will be enrolling in a Vancouver-area secondary school next year, or who will be graduating this summer, are eligible to come and attend. Any level of experience, from complete unfamiliarity to existing expertise, is welcome.

About the Course

First Class Ambitions

We will definitely make some fun and simple demo projects.   But overall, the class is about real fundamentals in formal computer science, and professional-level programming.   We will also work on leadership, presentation skills, and health and fitness: less painting-with-kids and more being-a-boss.

Getting it Done Together

The Bluesoxx program combines online materials from college-level computer science courses with personalized instruction by a local expert.   Most importantly, though, students work together to build and share expertise as a team, and take the lead in making this happen: not quite like joining a gang, but more than just taking a class.

Free Mt. Pleasant Classes

The May and June classes will be held after school at SuiteGenius Mount Pleasant , near the Broadway-City Hall Skytrain station.   Dates and times will be scheduled once registration is closed.   These introductory classes will be held free of charge; rates aligned with comparable skilled private lessons will be introduced in the summer.

Real Accomplishments

The ability to use software, like the ability to read 300 years ago, is transforming assumptions about what people can accomplish.   Early birds who progress from computer-literate to best-selling-authors of computation are not just guaranteeing the income or lifestyle they want--they have the opportunity to shape the world.
